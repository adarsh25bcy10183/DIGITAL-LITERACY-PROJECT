Summary: Digital literacy is the ability to use digital tools, platforms, and technologies effectively, critically, and responsibly. It involves skills such as searching for information online, evaluating sources, communicating through digital media, creating content, and understanding cybersecurity. In today’s interconnected world, digital literacy is essential for education, employment, and social participation. It empowers individuals to access government services, engage in the digital economy, and avoid risks like misinformation or privacy breaches. By bridging the digital divide, especially in rural areas, digital literacy ensures inclusion, opportunity, and growth in a rapidly evolving technological landscape.
