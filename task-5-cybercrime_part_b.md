# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students

# &#x20;General Online Safety

1. **Always verify the URL before entering login credentials.** Official college and bank websites use HTTPS. Look for the padlock icon in your browser address bar. If the URL looks slightly different (e.g., "vitbhopa1.ac.in" instead of "vitbhopal.ac.in"), do not enter any information.
2. **Never click on links sent via WhatsApp, SMS, or email claiming urgency.** Messages like "Your account will be suspended — click now" are classic phishing tactics. Always go directly to the official website by typing the URL yourself.
3. **Use strong, unique passwords for every account.** A strong password has at least 12 characters and includes uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across multiple platforms.
4. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on your email, social media, and academic portals wherever available. This adds a second layer of protection even if your password is compromised.
5. **Keep your devices and apps updated.** Software updates often include critical security patches. Enable automatic updates on your phone and laptop.
6. **Do not connect to unsecured public Wi-Fi** for sensitive activities like banking, logging into academic portals, or submitting forms. Use mobile data or a trusted VPN instead.

## &#x20;Financial \& UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

7. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV number with anyone**, including people claiming to be from your bank, Google Pay support, or a government helpline. Legitimate institutions will NEVER ask for these details.
8. **Beware of "collect request" scams on UPI.** Scammers send a payment collect request and tell you to "enter your PIN to receive money." Entering your UPI PIN on a collect request sends money — it does not receive it. Always read what you are approving before entering your PIN.

## &#x20;Social Media \& Identity Safety

9. **Do not post photos of your Aadhaar card, PAN card, college ID, or any government document** on social media or send them via WhatsApp to unknown contacts.
10. **Review your social media privacy settings every semester.** Ensure your phone number, email, and date of birth are not publicly visible.

## &#x20;Reporting Channels

If you are a victim of any cybercrime:

* **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File a complaint online for financial fraud, social media crimes, or identity theft.
* **Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930** (available 24×7, toll-free across India) — Call immediately if you have lost money to online fraud; quick action can help freeze fraudulent transactions.
* **Local Police:** File an FIR at your nearest police station and ask for it to be forwarded to the Cyber Crime Cell.
