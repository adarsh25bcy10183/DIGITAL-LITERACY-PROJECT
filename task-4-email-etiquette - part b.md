Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students


DO's

1. Do keep your profiles professional. Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that represents you positively. Recruiters and professors can and do look at social media.

2. Do think before you post. Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parents, or a future employer saw this?" If not, don't post it.

3. Do use privacy settings actively. Regularly review who can see your posts, stories, and personal information on every platform you use.

4. Do credit others' work. If you share someone else's photo, article, or idea, always give proper credit or attribution.

5. Do report cyberbullying or harassment. If you witness or experience online bullying, use the platform's reporting tools and inform a trusted adult or college authority.

6. Do verify information before sharing. Misinformation spreads fast on social media. Check the source before forwarding news, statistics, or health advice.

7. Do use two-factor authentication on all your social media accounts to protect them from unauthorised access.

---

  DON'Ts

1. Don't share personal contact information publicly. Your phone number, home address, college ID number, and Aadhaar details should never be posted publicly on any platform.

2. Don't share OTPs, passwords, or banking details — not even with someone claiming to be from your bank, college, or a government body.

3. Don't engage in or spread hate speech, trolling, or harassment. Online behaviour has real legal and academic consequences in India under the IT Act, 2000.

4. Don't overshare your daily routine or location. Posting "I'm alone at home till 8 PM" or tagging your real-time location can be a safety risk.

5. Don't use social media during exam time without boundaries. Create time-limited usage habits; excessive social media use is linked to reduced focus and academic performance.

6. Don't click on suspicious links sent via DMs, group chats, or email — especially those offering prizes, scholarships, or urgent account verification.
